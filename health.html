Benefits of Surya Namaskar

  • Improves flexibility and strength.
  • Reduces stress and anxiety.
  • Enhances respiratory function.
  • Boosts blood circulation.
  • Helps with weight management.

Quick Tips

  • Perform on an empty stomach.
  • Maintain a consistent pace.
  • Focus on your breath.
  • Find a quality teacher An experienced yoga instructor can provide a great deal of insight and guidance as you practice. A qualified yoga teacher can answer your questions during your class, as well as help you achieve the proper posture for each pose with individualized cues and hands-on adjustments. A great teacher should be knowledgeable yet kind, friendly, and compassionate.
  • Learn some poses ahead of time Many of the asanas or poses can feel complicated, especially at first. Luckily, you can spend some time before your first class getting accustomed to the poses that you can expect to perform, such as Mountain pose, Child, Warrior 1, Sun Salutations, and so on. By learning a few of these beginner poses, you’ll feel more confident and ready to take part in your class. Wear comfortable clothing The right clothes can make a world of difference in class. Ideally, you’ll want to select a supportive top and yoga pants made from breathable materials with moisture-wicking features. For cooler weather, you may want to invest in thermal athletic gear you can easily layer to stay warm.

What are the different steps in Surya Namaskar?

  1. Pranamasana

    2. The first pose in the Surya Namaskar is Pranamasana. Stand straight with your feet together, relax your chest, join your hands in a prayer pose, and breathe slowly to relax.

  2. Hastauttanasana

    3. In this pose, you keep your hands touched like in Prayer mode and stretch the body backward. Keep your biceps to the ears.

  3. Hastapadasana

    4. Touch your palms to the feet. Keep your spine erect, breathe in, and start stretching forward to take your hands to the feet.

  4. Ashwa Sanchalanasana

    5. Put both hands on the floor in line with your feet, take the left leg backward and stretch it. Bend the right leg and ensure the knee touches your chest.

  5. Dandasana

    6. This is a Plank pose where you must put all your pressure on your hands, similar to a push-up pose.

  6. Ashtanga Namaskara

    7. Touch eight body parts to the ground: feet, knees, groin, stomach, chest, chin, nose, and head. Breathe deeply and hold the position.

  7. Bhujangasana

    8. From Ashtanga Namaskara, stretch your chest upwards, keep your elbows straight, and stretch the shoulders as well.

  8. Adho Mukha Svanasana

    9. Extend your hips upwards and keep your hands on the floor, resembling a Reverse “V” alphabet.

  9. Ashwa Sanchalanasana

    10. Repeat the same as the fourth step, but with the opposite leg.

  10. Hasta Padasana

    11. Touch your palms to the feet again, as in the third step.

  11. Hasta Uttanasana

    12. Raise both your hands with open palms, stretch your spine, and look backward.

  12. Pranamasana

    13. Conclude with the prayer pose, standing straight in Namaste with your palms at the center of your chest.

History of Surya Namaskar

Surya Namaskar, or Sun Salutation, is a sequence of 12 powerful yoga poses. Besides being a great cardiovascular workout, Surya Namaskar is also known to have an immensely positive impact on the body and mind.
